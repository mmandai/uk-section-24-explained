Section 24 Explained

The mortgage interest restriction that changed landlord tax — how it works, who it hurts, and when a limited company makes sense.

Finance Act 2015Personal vs Ltd CoHigher rate taxpayersUpdated 2026

What is Section 24?

Section 24 of the Finance Act 2015 removed the right of individual landlords to deduct mortgage interest as a business expense. Instead they receive a flat 20% tax credit. Phased in 2017–2020, fully in effect since April 2020. For basic rate taxpayers the outcome is roughly the same. For higher and additional rate taxpayers, the tax bill increases significantly.

How it works

Your full rental income (minus allowable expenses, excluding interest) is added to your other income. You then receive a 20% credit on the interest — not a deduction.

When does incorporating make sense?

Incorporation makes financial sense when the annual Section 24 penalty exceeds the one-off incorporation costs. This typically applies to higher or additional rate taxpayers with significant mortgage debt and multiple properties who can leave profits inside the company.

Bottom line

Section 24 costs higher rate taxpayers approximately 20p extra per £1 of mortgage interest. Limited companies avoid this entirely — but dividend tax on extraction partially offsets the saving. The right answer depends on your income level, number of properties and whether you need the rental income now.

Frequently asked questions

Does Section 24 apply to limited companies?
No. Section 24 only applies to properties held in personal names. Limited companies deduct 100% of mortgage interest before corporation tax.
Does Section 24 apply to furnished holiday lets?
The FHL regime was abolished from April 2025. Properties previously qualifying as FHLs are now standard residential lettings and subject to Section 24.
Can I claim mortgage interest as an expense at all?
No — not as a deductible expense. You receive a 20% basic rate tax credit on the interest paid. On £10,000 interest that's £2,000 off your tax bill — not a deduction from income.
Does Section 24 affect CGT?
No. Section 24 only affects income tax on rental profits. Capital Gains Tax on disposal is separate. See the Flip Profit calculator for CGT modelling.
